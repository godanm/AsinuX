What is Donkey?

Donkey (Kazhutha — கழுதை · കഴുத) is a traditional South Indian card game popular in Tamil Nadu and Kerala. The name literally means "Donkey" — the player who fails to empty their hand last earns that title and is eliminated. It is a trick-taking game for 4 players using a standard 52-card deck, combining skill, memory, and strategy.

Also known regionally as Donkey, Kalutai, or Kattai, the game rewards players who can read the table, manage their hand efficiently, and use the Vettu (cut) tactically to force opponents into picking up cards.

Setup

  • 🃏
    Players & Deck 4 players, standard 52-card deck. No jokers.
  • 🤲
    Dealing All 52 cards are dealt evenly — each player receives 13 cards.
  • ♠
    First Lead The player holding the Ace of Spades leads the first trick. After that, the winner of each trick leads the next.

How a Trick Works

  • 1️⃣
    Leader plays a card The current leader places one card face-up. That card's suit becomes the lead suit for this trick.
  • 2️⃣
    Others must follow suit Every other player must play a card of the same suit if they have one. There is no rank comparison — suit-following is the only obligation.
  • ✂️
    Vettu — the Cut If a player has no cards of the lead suit, they may play any card from another suit. The player holding the highest lead-suit card must pick up all cards played in the trick.
  • ▶️
    Next trick If no one cuts, the trick cards are discarded and the leader leads again. If a cut happens, the cutter leads the next trick.

The Vettu (Cut) — Explained

The Vettu is the heart of Donkey. When you cut, the player holding the highest lead-suit card — not you — picks up all the cards on the table. This can instantly balloon their hand from a few cards to ten or more.

Unlike traditional trick-taking games, there is no trump suit. Any card from any other suit can cut. The only requirement is that you genuinely have no cards in the lead suit. Playing a cut when you actually have the lead suit is a foul.

Escaping the Round

Your primary goal is to empty your hand before the other players. As soon as you play your last card, you escape and earn points based on your finishing position.

Escaped players remain at the table as observers but cannot play cards. The game continues until only one player still holds cards — that player becomes the Donkey and is eliminated.

All players start with 10,000 points. Points accumulate across games. The Donkey is eliminated each round; the last surviving player wins the tournament.

Strategy Guide

Early Game (13–9 cards)

Focus on suit depletion. Identify which suits you have the fewest cards in and play those first. Becoming void in a suit early gives you cut opportunities throughout the mid and late game.

♟️

Lead depleted suits

When you lead, play cards from your shortest suit. This helps you void that suit faster and forces opponents to follow or cut.

👀

Watch who cuts what

When an opponent cuts, note the suit they played. That tells you they are void in the lead suit — avoid leading that suit against them later.

🎯

Target high-card holders

The player with the highest lead-suit card picks up on a cut. Lead a suit where your opponent holds high cards to dump the pickup on them.

🃏

Rank doesn't matter

A 2 of Hearts and an Ace of Hearts are equal in Donkey — both just follow the lead suit. Manage suit distribution, not rank.

Late Game (3 or fewer cards)

Play conservatively. Avoid leading a suit where someone might cut you back. If you are behind, a well-timed cut on the leader is your only lifeline.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut even if I have one card left in the lead suit?

No. You can only cut if you have zero cards of the lead suit. Playing a cut when you have the lead suit is a foul.

What happens if two people cut in the same trick?

Both cards are still played. The player holding the highest lead-suit card picks up all cards including both cuts.

How many rounds does a game last?

With 4 players, a game lasts 3 rounds (one elimination per round). Games typically take 10–20 minutes.

What is Rummy?

The Rummy on kazhutha.app is 13-card Indian Rummy — the most popular card game in India. Each player receives 13 cards and must arrange them into valid sequences and sets. The first player to complete a valid declaration wins. Everyone else scores penalty points for cards that remain unmelded.

The game uses two standard decks plus printed Jokers, and designates one random card rank as the Wild Joker each game.

Setup

  • 🀄
    Players & Deck 2, 4, or 6 players. Two standard decks (104 cards) plus 2 printed Jokers — 106 cards total.
  • 🤲
    Dealing Each player receives 13 cards. One card is turned face-up to form the open deck.
  • 🃏
    Wild Joker The rank of the face-up card becomes the Wild Joker rank. All four suits of that rank (e.g. all 7s) act as jokers — plus the printed Jokers (★).

Your Turn

  • 📥
    Draw Take the top card from either the closed deck (face-down) or the open deck (face-up, visible to all).
  • 📤
    Discard Place one card from your hand face-up onto the open deck. Your hand must always stay at 13 cards.
  • 🏳️
    Declare When all 13 cards are arranged into valid melds, discard your 14th card face-down to declare and win.

Melds — Sequences & Sets

You need at least 1 pure sequence (no jokers) and at least 2 sequences total for a valid declaration. Without these, the declaration is invalid.

Pure Sequence

3 or more cards of the same suit in consecutive rank order — no jokers.

  • ✅
    Valid5♥ 6♥ 7♥  ·  10♠ J♠ Q♠ K♠
  • ❌
    Invalid (joker used)5♥ [JKR] 7♥ — jokers make this an impure sequence

Impure Sequence

3 or more consecutive same-suit cards where one or more jokers substitute for missing cards.

Set

3 or 4 cards of the same rank from different suits. Jokers can substitute for missing suits.

The winner scores 0. All other players score the sum of their unmelded card points, capped at 80.

Strategy Tips

🎯

Pure sequence first

Start building your pure sequence before anything else. Without it, no declaration is valid — even a perfect hand fails.

🃏

Use jokers wisely

Save jokers for high-value sets or difficult sequences. Don't waste them filling low-point gaps you could build purely.

👀

Watch the open deck

The cards your opponent picks up reveal their hand. Avoid discarding cards that help them complete melds.

🏳️

Drop when far behind

If you have a terrible hand after 2–3 turns, a 40-pt middle drop beats a likely 80-pt invalid declaration.

What is Game 28?

Game 28 is a beloved South Indian trick-taking and bidding card game, widely played in Kerala and Tamil Nadu. The name comes from the total point value available in each hand — 28 points, distributed among four special cards: Jack (3 pts), Nine (2 pts), Ace (1 pt), and Ten (1 pt).

Four players form two teams of two, sitting across from each other. One team bids for how many of the 28 points they will collect; the other team tries to prevent them from reaching that total.

Setup

  • 👥
    Players & Teams 4 players in 2 teams of 2. Partners sit opposite each other.
  • 🃏
    The Deck 32 cards: 7, 8, 9, 10, J, Q, K, A of all 4 suits. Each player receives 8 cards.
  • 🏆
    Point Cards Only four cards carry points — Jack (3), Nine (2), Ace (1), Ten (1). Total in play: 28. All other cards (K, Q, 8, 7) score zero.

Bidding

Bidding starts at 16 (the minimum — a team needs more than half the 28 points). Players raise the bid until one team passes. The team with the highest bid must reach their declared total.

  • 🔢
    Bid Range 16 to 28. You can also bid "Double" (guaranteeing all 28 points) for higher stakes.
  • 🃏
    Trump Declaration After winning the bid, the bidding team privately decides the trump suit. Trump is only revealed when a player cannot follow the lead suit.

How a Trick Works

  • 1️⃣
    Lead a card The current leader plays one card face-up; all others must follow the same suit if they can.
  • ♠
    Trump beats all If you can't follow suit, you may play a trump card. Trump always beats any non-trump card; highest trump wins the trick.
  • 🏆
    Trick winner Highest card of the lead suit wins (if no trump was played). Trick winner leads the next trick.

Point Values

CardPoints

Nine (9)2 pts
Ace (A)1 pt

Total in play28 pts

Winning the Round

At the end of all 8 tricks, both teams count their collected points. If the bidding team reaches their declared total, they win the round. If they fall short, the defending team wins.

App Scoring

OutcomePoints
Win a round+30 pts
Lose a round−20 pts
Win the game+100 pts bonus

Strategy Tips

🃏

Protect your Jacks

The Jack is worth 3 points — more than any other card. Lead with lower cards in the same suit to draw out opponents' trumps before playing your Jack.

🤫

Delay revealing trump

As the bidding team, hold trump as long as possible. Revealing it too early lets the defenders avoid leading into it.

🤝

Signal your partner

Lead a point card you don't need when your partner is winning a trick — it signals them to take the trick and keeps the points in your team.

🛡️

Defend by splitting point tricks

As the defending team, aim to ensure point-heavy tricks fall across both your players so the bidding team can't collect enough to meet their bid.

What is Teen Patti?

Teen Patti (तीन पत्ती · "Three Cards") is India's most popular gambling card game, often called the Indian Poker. Players are dealt 3 cards each and bet on who holds the strongest hand. The game blends luck, strategy, and the art of reading your opponents.

On kazhutha.app, you play against bot opponents. The pot grows with each round of betting — the player with the best hand at showdown, or the last one standing after all others fold, wins the pot.

Setup

  • 🎴
    Deck & Players Standard 52-card deck (no jokers). 2–6 players, each dealt 3 cards face-down.
  • 💰
    Boot (Ante) All players put a fixed boot amount into the pot before cards are dealt. This is the minimum stake.
  • 🙈
    Blind vs Seen You can choose to play blind (bet without looking at your cards, half the cost) or seen (after looking, double the current stake).

Hand Rankings

Highest to lowest: Trail → Pure Sequence → Sequence → Color → Pair → High Card

Color (Flush)Three same-suit cards (non-consecutive)2♣ 7♣ K♣

High CardNone of the aboveA♠ 7♥ 3♦

How to Play

  • 💸
    Chaal (Call) Match the current bet to stay in the round. Seen players pay double the blind bet amount.
  • 🏳️
    Fold Give up your hand and forfeit your bets. Folding ends your participation in this round.
  • 🎯
    Show (Sideshow) Request to compare cards with the previous player. The lower hand folds. Both must be "seen" players to request a show.
  • 🏆
    Winning If all but one player folds, that player wins the pot. Otherwise, the player with the highest-ranking hand at showdown wins.

App Scoring

Your in-app points reflect your share of the pot when you win a round. Fold to protect your points; stay in when you have a strong hand or a good read on your opponents.

Strategy Tips

🙈

Play blind longer

Playing blind costs half — it projects confidence and keeps your options open without giving away whether you looked at your cards.

📊

Pot odds matter

If the pot is large relative to the call amount, even a moderate hand is worth staying in. Don't fold prematurely when the value is there.

😐

Be consistent

Against bots, vary your bet sizing regardless of hand strength. Predictable betting patterns (big = strong, small = weak) are easy to exploit.

🏳️

Fold weak hands early

High Card and weak Pairs rarely win multi-player showdowns. Fold early to limit losses; the pot will be there next round.

What is Blackjack?

Blackjack (also known as 21) is the world's most popular casino card game. Your goal is simple: build a hand closer to 21 than the dealer without going over. On kazhutha.app, you play against a dealer bot using standard casino rules.

Unlike most card games, Blackjack is a game of you vs the house — there are no other player hands to beat. The basic strategy is well-established, and following it consistently is the most effective way to play.

Card Values

CardValue
2 through 10Face value (2 = 2, 10 = 10)
Jack, Queen, King10 each
Ace1 or 11 (whichever benefits you)

How to Play

  • 🃏
    Deal You receive 2 cards face-up. The dealer receives 2 cards — one face-up, one face-down (the hole card).
  • 👆
    Hit Draw one more card. You can hit as many times as you like, but going over 21 is a Bust — you lose immediately.
  • ✋
    Stand End your turn with your current hand. The dealer then plays.
  • ✌️
    Double Down Double your bet and receive exactly one more card — then you must stand. Best used when you have 10 or 11.
  • 🏦
    Dealer Rules The dealer must hit until reaching 17 or higher. The dealer stands on 17 (including Soft 17). No choice — dealer plays by fixed rules.

Blackjack — Special Hand

A Blackjack is an Ace + any 10-value card on your first two cards. It pays 2× your bet and beats the dealer's 21 (unless the dealer also has Blackjack — that's a push).

Payouts

ResultPayout
Blackjack (A + 10-value on first 2 cards)+2× bet
Win (beat dealer without busting)+1× bet
Push (tie)Bet returned
Bust or dealer beats you−1× bet

Basic Strategy

📉

Hard 8 or less: always Hit

You can't bust by drawing one card on 11 or less. Hit until you reach a standing total.

✋

Hard 17+: always Stand

The risk of busting outweighs the potential gain. Stand and let the dealer take the risk.

✌️

Double Down on 10 or 11

Against a dealer showing 2–9, doubling down on 10 or 11 maximises expected value since you're likely to land a strong hand.

🃏

Soft hand: be aggressive

A soft hand (with an Ace counting as 11) can't bust on one draw. Hit or double more freely on soft 13–17.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a "soft" hand?

A hand containing an Ace counted as 11. E.g., Ace + 6 = Soft 17. It's "soft" because drawing a high card just flips the Ace to 1 instead of busting you.

What happens if dealer and I both get Blackjack?

It's a push — your bet is returned. Neither player wins.

Can I double down after hitting?

No. Double Down is only available on your first two cards.

Beat the dealer. Don't bust. Simple as that.

What is Bluff?

Bluff (also known as Cheat or I Doubt It) is a social deception card game where players take turns placing cards face-down while claiming a specific rank — but they can lie. The goal is to be the first to empty your hand, by bluffing convincingly or by catching others in the act.

It is one of the few card games where your worst cards can be your most powerful weapons — any card can pass for any rank if no one calls your bluff.

Setup

  • 🃏
    Deck & Players Standard 52-card deck. 2–6 players; cards are dealt as evenly as possible.
  • 🔢
    Starting Rank The game starts with rank 2. Each turn the declared rank increments — 2, 3, 4 … K, A, then back to 2.
  • 🎯
    Goal Empty your hand before everyone else. The first player to play their last card wins.

How to Play

  • 🃏
    Your Turn Play 1–4 cards face-down onto the discard pile and announce how many cards of the current rank you played (e.g. "Two Fives"). You can lie.
  • 🤫
    Calling Bluff Any player can call "Bluff!" before the next turn. The played cards are revealed:
  • 😳
    If you were bluffing You pick up the entire discard pile and add it to your hand.
  • 😤
    If you were honest The player who called bluff picks up the entire discard pile.
  • ▶️
    No call If nobody calls bluff, the cards stay in the pile and the next player takes their turn with the next rank.

Winning

The first player to play all their cards wins. Note: if your last play is called — and you were bluffing — you must pick up the pile. The game continues until your last honest play goes unchallenged.

Strategy Tips

🎭

Bluff on ranks you partly hold

If you play one genuine 5 and one bluff card claiming "two fives," a reveal still shows at least one honest card — harder to catch.

📦

Call when pile is big

A wrong call costs you a huge pile. Wait for a player who dumps 3–4 cards of a rank they've already exhausted earlier in the game.

🏃

Dump cards in groups

When it's your rank, play as many as you can — real and bluffed — to reduce your hand quickly and pressure others to call or let it slide.

🧠

Track played ranks

Each rank has 4 cards. If you've seen three 7s already, a player claiming two more 7s is almost certainly bluffing. Call it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I play zero cards on my turn?

No. You must play at least one card each turn, honest or bluffed.

What if my last card is called and I was bluffing?

You pick up the entire pile and the game continues — you haven't won yet.

Can multiple people call bluff?

Only one player's call is actioned — the first one to call. In the app, any player can call before the next turn begins.
